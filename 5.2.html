    HappyFamily.

    Chinese New Year Eve in Zhonghe, Taipei. Extended family gathered around 13 scrumptious dishes on a traditional circular table. Everyone wearing bright red attire.

    Enter A-yi.
    A-yi [a large smile]: Have you eaten?!?
    Enter Shu-shu.
    Shu-shu [lighthearted]: It’s been 5 years since the last time you’ve come back!! Are you going to work in Canada?
    A-yi [eyes enlarging, speaking passionately]: Chemical engineering!! Going to be a doctor?
    Shu-shu [smiling modestly]: Lots of money, your son’s very successful!! My son is pursuing law school in America!
    Shu-shu [actually, a not-so-genuine smile]: Now how much are you getting paid?
    Exit drunk Shu-shu.
    A-yi [slightly irritated]: Specifically how much?
    A-yi: So have you found yourself a girlfriend?
    A-yi [eyes narrowing, head tilting up]: When are you getting married?
    Exit frustrated A-yi.

    Your mother waited her whole life to give birth to you.
    To a son.
    Humiliated by family, right at this exact same dinner table.

    As Bruce Lee once said, “I think of myself as a human being, because under the sky we are but one family, it just so happens we look different.”
